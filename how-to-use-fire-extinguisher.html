How to Use a Fire Extinguisher

The PASS method explained clearly, before adrenaline turns your brain into soup.

Use the PASS Method

PASS stands for Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep. If you can remember those four words, you can remember the basic process under stress.

P

Pull

Pull the pin to break the tamper seal and unlock the handle.

A

Aim

Aim low at the base of the fire, not at the flames above it.

S

Squeeze

Squeeze the handle to discharge the extinguishing agent.

S

Sweep

Sweep side to side across the base until the fire is out.

Important Kitchen Fire Rules

  • Only fight small fires. If the fire is spreading, producing heavy smoke, or blocking your exit, get out and call 911.
  • Keep the exit behind you. Never let the fire get between you and your way out.
  • Do not use water on grease fires. It can cause an explosive spread of burning oil.
  • Shut off the heat if you safely can. But do not reach through flames to do it.
  • Watch for re-ignition. Fires can flare back up after they appear extinguished.

When a Fire Blanket Is Better

For a small pan fire, a fire blanket is often easier and cleaner than an extinguisher. You can place it over the pan to smother the fire without blasting powder across the kitchen.

Practice matters: Everyone in the house should know where the extinguisher is, what type it is, and how to use it. The first time you touch it should not be during an actual fire.
