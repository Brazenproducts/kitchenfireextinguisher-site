Fire Extinguisher Types Explained

ABC, Class K, CO2, and water: what they do and which one belongs in your kitchen.

In This Guide

  1. ABC Dry Chemical
  2. Class K Wet Chemical
  3. CO2
  4. Water
  5. Best Type for Kitchens

ABC Dry Chemical

ABC extinguishers are the default recommendation for homes because they cover Class A fires like paper or wood, Class B fires involving flammable liquids, and Class C energized electrical fires. In plain English: they can handle a wide range of kitchen-adjacent problems.

That is why most of our top picks are ABC units. They are versatile and practical. The main drawback is cleanup. The powder gets everywhere and can be hard on appliances and electronics.

Class K Wet Chemical

Class K extinguishers are purpose-built for cooking oils and fats. They cool the fire and create a foam-like layer that helps stop re-ignition. This is the extinguisher type commercial kitchens rely on for fryer and grease-heavy cooking environments.

For homes, Class K is excellent when you deep fry regularly or want specialized protection for oil fires. It is usually not the only extinguisher you should own unless your risk profile is very kitchen-specific.

CO2 Extinguishers

CO2 extinguishers are clean and effective on electrical fires and some flammable liquid fires. They do not leave residue, which makes them popular around electronics and server rooms.

For kitchens, CO2 is usually not the first recommendation because it does not cool cooking oil the way Class K does, and its short discharge time gives you less room for error.

Water Extinguishers

Water extinguishers are for ordinary combustibles only. They are not for grease fires. They are not for energized electrical fires. They do not belong as your main kitchen extinguisher.

This is the part people get wrong in a panic: throwing water on burning oil can create a violent flare-up that spreads the fire instantly.

So What Should Be in a Kitchen?

For most homes: ABC

An ABC extinguisher is the best single answer for a residential kitchen because it covers the broadest range of likely household fire scenarios.

For heavy oil cooking: Add Class K

If you deep fry often, keep a Class K extinguisher nearby too. It is the more specialized, more technically correct tool for cooking-oil fires.

Also keep a fire blanket

A fire blanket is one of the smartest additions you can make. For a small pan fire, it is often the fastest and cleanest response.
