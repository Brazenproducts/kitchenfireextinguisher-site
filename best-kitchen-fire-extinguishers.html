Kitchen Fire Extinguisher Buying Guide

ABC is the default answer for most homes

If you only buy one extinguisher for a residential kitchen, make it a rechargeable ABC unit from a reputable brand. It gives you the widest protection and the fewest edge cases.

Class K makes sense when oil is a big part of your cooking

If you deep fry often, run a powerful gas range, or want the right chemistry for grease fires specifically, add a Class K extinguisher. It is the specialized answer, not always the only answer.

Do not hide the extinguisher under the sink behind chemicals

Mount it near the kitchen exit path so you can reach it while moving away from the fire, not toward it. You want clear access and a clean escape route.

Safety tip: Pair your extinguisher with a fire blanket. For a small pan fire, a blanket is often faster, cleaner, and less intimidating for family members.

FAQ

Is a fire extinguisher enough for a kitchen?

No. You should also have working smoke alarms, a fire blanket, and a plan to get out. If the fire is growing, leave and call 911.

Can I use water on a grease fire?

No. Water can violently spread burning oil. Use a lid, baking soda for very small incidents, a fire blanket, or the correct extinguisher.

Where should I mount a kitchen extinguisher?

Near the exit route, not right next to the stove. You want to access it without leaning over the fire.
