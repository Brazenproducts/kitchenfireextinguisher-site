Why Kitchen Fire Safety Matters

#1 Cause of Home Fires

Cooking fires account for nearly half of all home fires in the US. Having the right extinguisher within reach can mean the difference between a small incident and a total loss.

Seconds Count

A grease fire doubles in size every 30 seconds. You don't have time to search for the right tool — it needs to be accessible, charged, and the correct type for kitchen fires.

Water Makes It Worse

Throwing water on a grease fire causes a fireball. Most people don't know this until it's too late. The right extinguisher (Class B or K) smothers the fire safely.

UL Certification Matters

Not all extinguishers are created equal. We only recommend UL-listed products that have been independently tested and certified for the fire classes they claim.

Our Take

Most kitchen fires are preventable, and the ones that aren't are manageable — if you have the right equipment within arm's reach. The problem is that most people buy whatever fire extinguisher is cheapest at the hardware store without checking if it's rated for grease fires (Class B) or cooking oil fires (Class K). We built this site because the difference between the right and wrong extinguisher can be catastrophic. Every guide starts with what you actually need before we tell you what to buy.
