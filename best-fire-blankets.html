Why every kitchen should have a fire blanket

  • No-mess response: You can smother a small pan fire without filling your kitchen with dry chemical powder.
  • Low intimidation factor: People who freeze at the idea of using an extinguisher will often use a blanket.
  • Great for small stovetop incidents: Blankets shine before the fire spreads beyond the cookware.
  • Works alongside an extinguisher: A blanket complements a real extinguisher. It does not replace one.

What to look for

Clear pull tabs

If deployment is not instantly obvious, it is a worse product.

Reasonable size

Most home blankets are sized around small-kitchen needs. Bigger can be better, but wall storage and ease of deployment still matter.

Good placement

Mount it where you can reach it while backing away from the stove. Same rule as extinguishers.

Best setup: Keep both a rechargeable ABC extinguisher and a fire blanket in the kitchen. The blanket handles small pan fires cleanly. The extinguisher is your backup when things are already getting ugly.
